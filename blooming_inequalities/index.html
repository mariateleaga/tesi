The taller the stem, the richer the country: height reflects GDP per capita.

The further right a flower stands, the more CO2 it emits per person, every year.

Grey flowers are the countries most vulnerable to climate change today. The rare ones with a small leaf are the “model” cases — wealthy nations that kept their emissions low.

Many countries — like the United States and China — emitted enormously over the decades, and grew rich doing it. Others — like Ethiopia and Rwanda — have emitted almost nothing, and still struggle to grow at all.
